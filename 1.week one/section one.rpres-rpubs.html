Features of Religion

  • Primordial

    • “Primordial identity is based on features that cannot be changed or questioned, they appear to be given by nature”

  • Constructive

    • Religions build collective preferences.

  • Instrumental

    • Actors can use religion as a political and social tool

Bedi: What is so special about religion?

  • Main Point: Religion must be seen as a continuum.

+“We can characterize religious practices on a continuum. On one end stands the conception of religion as a priori and given-there is no choice in being Sikh or Christian(…) The other end contends that all religious affiliations and practices are chosen making the very notion of a coherent community a fiction”_ (pp. 236)

  • One side, Religious practices are primordial. In the other, they can open space for constructivism, and instrumentalism.

  • Just recognizing religion as primordial that one has normative argument to justify political exemptions to religious practices.
