What is a Research Question?

  1. What is this paper about?

  2. Why is this paper important?

  3. What is a well-written research question?

  • Make it as a question
  • Present it in the first paragraph of your paper
  • Clear, simple, intuitive, cohesive

Think about X's and Y's.

What is a literature review?

The literature review can serve to multiple purposes. Some of my favorite:

  1. What do other authors talk about your topic?

  2. How did a literature help your to organize your questions?

  3. Are you filling a gap in the literature? Proposing a new explanation? Adding a new case?

What is not a literature review?

Some common mistakes:

  1. The discussion about your case is not a literature review

  2. The literature review is not a set of bullet points about the readings you did. You should connect the authors' ideas.

Finally, general instructions:

  1. You should cite the authors properly.

  2. You can and should use the readings we did.

  3. You can and should add new readings. Please, ask me for suggestions.

Hypothesis

  1. What are your expectations for the research question?

  2. The relationship between Xs and Ys is positive, negative, or neutral?

  3. What are the effects of the phenomena you are discussing in the paper?

Don't spend so long explaining the causes of your expectations. This will be in the argument section of the paper

Argument/Theory

The goal of this section is explain the reasoning behind your hypothesis.

Your hypothesis connects X and Y. Your argument should explain how those factors are connected.

Mechanisms, process, history.

Components Part II

Research Design

How do you plan to prove your hypothesis?

Options:

  1. Case-Study. Good for causal effects, detailed explanations. Bad for generalizations

  2. Comparative Case Study: Two or more cases. One case over time

  3. Quantitative data. Survey data, administrative data, figures, maps

Main Idea: Convince the reader you are using the best method correct.

To Finish the paper

Evidence: Implement your research design. Present as much information as you can.

Alternative Explanation What are rival hypothesis to your paper? Why do you think your explanation is more plausible

Conclusion: Re-estate your argument and goals of the paper. Present your hypothesis again. Summarize the evidences, and then discuss the importance of your findings.

Revise and Finalize the paper: Incorporate Part I and Part II together. Proofread. Organize Citations.
